# Future Goals & Vision: The Autonomous Desktop Companion

This document outlines the future roadmap for the Quantum VRM ecosystem, advancing beyond a simple conversational AI into a robust, real-time desktop assistant that integrates deeply with the user's workflow.

## 1. Real-Time Desktop Assistant App

The primary long-term goal is to build an application that assists the user in real-time, functioning directly on their computer. Unlike a browser-based chatbot, this will act as an always-on, intelligent companion capable of observing, analyzing, and helping with day-to-day tasks contextually.

**Key capabilities will include:**

- Real-time workflow observation.
- Instant contextual suggestions based on on-screen information.
- A persistently available, emotionally intelligent 3D VRoid avatar overlay.

## 2. Autonomous Data Research

The assistant will be able to perform fully automated data research using the user's own computer resources (e.g., controlling a headless Chrome instance or integrating with search engine APIs).

**Use Cases:**

- **Automating the Mundane:** The assistant can independently crawl the web for specific information needed for future presentations, saving hours of manual labor.
- **Deep Dives & Fact-Checking:** Compiling reports, summarizing long articles, and fetching references in real-time while the user works on other things.

## 3. Presentation Preparation & Question Generation

Leveraging the gathered research data, the assistant will be able to actively prepare materials.

**Key features:**

- **Automated Slides:** Programmatically generating presentation slides based on the synthesized research.
- **Interactive Questioning:** Generating a list of anticipated questions and answers to help the user prepare for a meeting or presentation.
- **Real-Time Coaching:** During a mock presentation, the assistant can provide feedback or pose questions dynamically based on the topic.

## Conclusion

The ultimate vision is an AI that handles the nuanced, boring, and time-consuming aspects of modern work—transforming the user's computer into a proactive, intelligent partner.
